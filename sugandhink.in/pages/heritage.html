The Maison's
Heritage

Born from a reverence for the ancient art of Indian perfumery, Sugandh Ink is a living homage to the Kannauj artisans who first distilled the soul of the earth into glass.

Kannauj · Uttar Pradesh
01 / 04

The Deg-Bhapka
Method

For over five centuries, the artisans of Kannauj have practised Deg-Bhapka hydro-distillation - a copper still, a fire of sandalwood, and an open sky. Petals are packed into the Deg, water is added, and the sealed vessel rests over a slow flame.

Steam carrying the botanical essence travels through a bamboo pipe into the receiver - the Bhapka - submerged in cool water. Here, the vapour condenses into attar. The process demands twelve to sixteen hours of uninterrupted attention for a single harvest.

At Sugandh Ink, we source exclusively from these ateliers, refusing the shortcut of synthetic aroma chemicals. Each composition begins with this truth.

Base Wood Aging · 12 Months
02 / 04

The Sandalwood
Foundation

All traditional attars are received into old sandalwood oil - a living base that absorbs and integrates the distilled essence over months. The sandalwood acts not merely as a carrier but as a collaborator, deepening and rounding the botanical character.

Our Oud Royal rests in Mysore sandalwood for twelve full months before being sealed. No synthetic fixatives, no alcohol. Only time and wood.

Malad West · Mumbai
03 / 04

Founded in
Mumbai

Sugandh Ink was established in 2026 in Malad West, Mumbai - a city of layered sensory experience. The maison bridges the ancient knowledge of Kannauj with a contemporary sensibility shaped by the diversity and energy of India's commercial capital.

Each fragrance in our library is a dialogue between heritage and modernity: the botanical truth of the attar tradition expressed through compositions designed for the contemporary Indian life - office mornings, monsoon evenings, and quiet midnight hours.

Ethical Sourcing · Wild-Harvested
04 / 04

Botanical
Integrity

We maintain direct relationships with growers across Rajasthan (rose and jasmine), Kerala (vetiver and pepper), Assam (agarwood), and the Nilgiris (cardamom and eucalyptus). Every harvest is traceable. Every relationship is long-term.

We categorically refuse the use of synthetic aroma chemicals in our core attar line. For our contemporary perfume compositions, we work only with IFRA-approved naturals and the minimum necessary of safe synthetic musks - disclosed fully on every product page.

A Chronology

The milestones that shaped the Maison.

500 BCE

The First Distillation

Ancient texts describe the preparation of Sura and Asava through distillation in Kannauj - the original perfume city of India, then known as Kanyakubja.

1600s

The Mughal Patronage

Empress Noor Jahan is credited with discovering the process of extracting Rose attar. The Mughal court elevated Kannauj perfumers to royal status, cementing the Deg-Bhapka tradition.

2025

The First Composition

Sugandh Ink's founding nose travels to Kannauj for six months, studying under third-generation attar makers. Oud Royal - the first original composition - takes form.

2026

The Maison Opens

Sugandh Ink launches in Mumbai with a library of twenty-nine compositions spanning attars, contemporary perfumes, and one-of-a-kind bespoke creations.
